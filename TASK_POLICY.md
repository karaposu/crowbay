# Crowdjump Task Policy — What We Allow and Why

Crowdjump is a marketplace where verified humans ("Jumpers") perform digital
tasks for pay, with completion proven by screen recording. This page states —
plainly — which tasks we host, which we refuse, and who carries which risk.
It is published because we would rather be judged on an honest policy than
trusted on a vague one.

## What we always refuse

These never launch, regardless of pay or intent:

- **Deception presented as genuine** — fake reviews, "say you bought it,"
  fake personas, fabricated testimonials. Real people doing real tasks is the
  platform's foundation; pretending is not work we sell.
- **Anything illegal or harmful** — fraud, harassment, targeting individuals.
- **Account handover and access mechanics** — using a task-poster's login,
  farming captchas, creating accounts to hand over. Jumpers only ever work
  from their own accounts on public surfaces.
- **Paid public reviews** — even "honest" ones. A paid review posted to a
  public review surface (app stores, map listings, marketplaces) cannot carry
  the disclosure that consumer-protection law requires the reading public to
  see, and buying a particular sentiment is prohibited outright. If you want
  product impressions, ask for a **private-feedback task** — Jumpers send
  their honest impressions to you instead of posting publicly. We suggest
  this rewrite automatically.
- **Unsolicited mass contact** — DM or comment campaigns aimed at strangers
  at scale. Recipients didn't opt into your promotion.

## What we hold for human review

- **Political or advocacy engagement** — paid engagement on political,
  electoral, or advocacy content is paused for a human look before it can
  launch. Coordinated influence is a line we check case by case rather than
  automate.

## What we allow — with both eyes open

**Engagement tasks** (likes, follows, subscribes, views, profile visits by
real humans acting from their own accounts, with no false claims) are
allowed. We state the conflict honestly rather than hiding it:

- **Most platforms' rules prohibit incentivized engagement.** A like bought
  here may be removed in a platform purge, and platforms may act against
  accounts that perform paid engagement.
- **If you launch such a task**, you are choosing that risk: purges can
  remove the results you paid for. The task card tells you this before you
  launch. Refund treatment for purged engagement is published with the
  payments component.
- **If you perform such a task**, your platform account carries the risk.
  Crowdjump shows a risk notice on every such task, asks you to acknowledge
  it once before your first one, and gives you a feed setting to hide
  account-risk tasks entirely. Performing them is always your choice; pay is
  never contingent on hiding what you did.

**Platform-neutral digital work** — testing, translation, form-filling,
research, private feedback — has no such conflict and carries no notices.

## How this policy is operated

- Every task is screened before launch by our task consumer (an AI reviewer
  plus deterministic checks); its category decision is logged on every task.
- Any category on this page can be **paused instantly** if a platform objects
  or enforcement patterns change; affected posters and performers are
  notified.
- Jumpers can **report any task** that seems to violate this page; reports go
  to a human.
- This policy carries explicit revisit triggers: legal counsel review, the
  first formal platform contact, payment-rail or app-store expansion, and
  observed purge/ban rates. It is versioned and will change in the open.

## What this page is not

It is not encouragement. Crowdjump sells verified human work, not metrics.
If your goal is durable reach, platform-neutral work and your own content
will serve you better than purchased signals — we host engagement tasks
because real adults on both sides can weigh that trade themselves, with the
facts stated.
