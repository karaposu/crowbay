# Trust Score Algorithm

## What it is and why it matters
A dynamic scoring system that quantifies user trustworthiness based on verification completeness, task performance history, and platform behavior. This creates a reputation economy where good actors are rewarded with better opportunities while problematic users face natural consequences.

## How this concept helps the overall project
- **Quality incentive** - Motivates performers to maintain high standards
- **Risk mitigation** - Identifies potentially problematic users early
- **Automated privileges** - High scores unlock premium features
- **Market confidence** - Posters prefer high-trust performers
- **Self-regulation** - Community polices itself through scores

## How this concept limits the overall project
- **Gaming vulnerability** - Users may try manipulating scores
- **Recovery difficulty** - Damaged scores hard to rebuild
- **New user disadvantage** - Start with low scores limiting opportunities
- **Algorithmic bias** - May inadvertently favor certain user types
- **Transparency balance** - Too much detail enables gaming

## What kind of information this concept needs as input
- Verification tier and completeness
- Task success/rejection rates
- Response times and reliability
- Poster ratings and feedback
- Dispute history
- Platform rule violations
- Account age and consistency
- Screen recording quality

## What kind of process this concept should use
1. **Component scoring** - Calculate sub-scores for each factor
2. **Weighted combination** - Merge scores based on importance
3. **Time decay** - Recent behavior weighs more heavily
4. **Normalization** - Ensure fair comparison across users
5. **Threshold mapping** - Define privilege levels
6. **Anomaly detection** - Flag unusual changes
7. **Periodic recalculation** - Update based on new data

## What kind of information this concept outputs or relays
- Overall trust score (0-100)
- Component breakdown
- Score trend visualization
- Percentile ranking
- Unlocked privileges
- Improvement recommendations
- Risk flags for platform
- Score impact previews

## Good expected outcome of realizing this concept
Trust scores become a valuable asset that users actively protect and improve. High-trust performers command premium rates and get first access to lucrative tasks. The algorithm fairly rewards consistent quality work while allowing redemption for past mistakes. Transparency builds user confidence without enabling manipulation. The system creates a virtuous cycle of quality improvement.

## Bad unwanted outcome of realizing this concept
The scoring algorithm becomes a source of anxiety and frustration as users obsess over minor fluctuations. Wealthy users game the system by buying their way to high scores. Cultural biases in the algorithm disadvantage certain groups. Score manipulation schemes proliferate. Users with low scores get trapped in a cycle where they can't access opportunities to improve. The system becomes punitive rather than encouraging.
